TERMS AND CONDITIONS

A division of IRM Enterprise

These Terms and Conditions outline the agreement between IRM Creditors and any loan applicant using its services. All applicants must read and understand the terms before submitting a loan request.

1. ELIGIBILITY

To qualify for a loan from IRM Creditors:

  • Applicant must be a citizen of Malawi
  • Applicant must be 18 years or older
  • A clear photo of a valid Malawi National ID must be submitted (passports not accepted)
  • Collateral must be submitted and approved before loan approval

Proof of income is not required. Exceptions to citizenship may be considered but are not guaranteed.

2. COLLATERAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Collateral must be submitted and verified before any loan is disbursed
  • The value of the collateral must equal or exceed the loan amount
  • Acceptable forms include electronics, motorcycles, appliances, or titled property, subject to evaluation
  • IRM Creditors reserves full discretion over acceptance of collateral

Important: Failure to repay the loan within the agreed time results in permanent forfeiture of the collateral. No return, exchange, or compensation shall be provided once collateral is seized.

3. LOAN DURATION AND REPAYMENT SCHEDULE

Loan repayment period is fixed at four (4) weeks from the date of disbursement. The repayment schedule is as follows:

Week 1 25%

of the loan amount

Week 2 35%

Week 4 75%

Any applicable service charges or interest will be stated clearly in the loan agreement.

4. REPAYMENT TERMS

  • Repayments must be made on or before the weekly deadlines
  • Early repayment is allowed and may reduce remaining charges if outlined in the agreement
  • Accepted payment methods include mobile money, direct bank deposit, or other approved options
  • Late payments may attract penalties and affect eligibility for future loans

5. DEFAULT AND CONSEQUENCES

A loan is considered in default if:

  • Full repayment is not completed within 30 days
  • Two or more scheduled payments are missed without prior notice

Consequences of default include:

  • Seizure and permanent retention of collateral
  • Application of penalties or recovery fees
  • Reporting to relevant credit reference bureaus
  • Engagement of debt collection agents
  • Ineligibility for future loan applications

6. APPLICANT RESPONSIBILITIES

Applicants are responsible for:

  • Submitting truthful and accurate information
  • Ensuring collateral is legally owned and unencumbered
  • Repaying the full loan within the agreed timeframe
  • Cooperating during any review or recovery process

7. PRIVACY AND DATA USE

  • Personal information is collected only for processing and managing loan applications
  • Data is not sold to third parties
  • Information may be shared with legal or recovery agents when required
  • All data is handled in line with applicable Malawian data protection laws

8. LOAN APPROVAL

Loan approval is based on internal evaluation criteria. Submission of an application does not guarantee approval. IRM Creditors reserves the right to approve, decline, or cancel any application at its sole discretion.

9. USE OF LOAN FUNDS

Loan funds must be used for lawful purposes. IRM Creditors is not liable for how the funds are used once disbursed.

10. RIGHT TO AMEND TERMS

IRM Creditors may revise these Terms and Conditions at any time. Updated terms will be posted on the website or communicated directly. Continued use of services implies acceptance of the revised terms.

11. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

Disputes will first be addressed through IRM Creditors' internal process. If unresolved, matters may be referred to a legal mediator or handled in a court within Malawian jurisdiction.

12. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

IRM Creditors is not liable for:

  • Indirect or incidental losses related to service use
  • Delays caused by third-party payment systems
  • Service interruptions due to maintenance or technical issues

13. ENTIRE AGREEMENT

These Terms constitute the full and binding agreement between IRM Creditors and the applicant. No informal or verbal agreements shall be enforceable unless documented in writing.
